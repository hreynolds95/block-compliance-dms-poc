## Overview

**Introduction and Purpose:** This Standard establishes the financial crimes compliance program for Squareup Europe Ltd. (SEL), covering anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and sanctions obligations applicable to SEL's operations.

**Scope:** Applies to Squareup Europe Ltd. and its regulated activities under applicable EU and UK financial crimes regulations.

**Definitions:**

| Terminology | Definition |
|---|---|
| **AML** | Anti-Money Laundering |
| **CTF** | Counter-Terrorist Financing |
| **MLRO** | Money Laundering Reporting Officer |
| **Staff Member(s)** | An employee or external worker hired by the Company who supports the operations covered by this Standard |

## [Brand/Entity]-Specific Requirements

### Square

SEL operates the Square point-of-sale product in the EU/UK. Financial crimes controls apply to all merchant onboarding, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting for Square merchants within SEL's regulatory perimeter.

## Compliance & Enforcement

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Block, Inc. Roles:**

- **Financial Crimes Compliance (FCC) Team:** Owns global financial crimes standards; provides oversight and guidance to SEL
- **Chief Compliance Officer:** Escalation point for material financial crimes issues

**Subsidiary Roles:**

- **SEL MLRO:** Responsible for AML/CTF program compliance within SEL; files Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- **SEL Compliance Team:** Implements program requirements; conducts ongoing monitoring

### Monitoring and Reporting

On an ongoing basis, the SEL MLRO monitors transaction activity, SAR filings, and regulatory developments. Quarterly reporting is provided to the SEL Board and Block FCC leadership.

### Record Retention

All documents are retained according to the longer of Block's Data Classification & Handling Policy, applicable EU/UK AML regulations (minimum 5 years), or other applicable regulatory requirements.

### Exceptions

Exception requests must be documented in writing and approved by the SEL MLRO and FCC leadership. Approved exceptions must be submitted to the Policy Governance Team.
