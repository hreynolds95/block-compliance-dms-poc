## Overview

**Introduction and Purpose:** This Compliance Management System (CMS) Policy establishes Block, Inc.'s enterprise-wide framework for managing compliance obligations across all business lines and legal entities.

**Scope:** Applies to Block, Inc., its subsidiaries, and affiliates, including all products.

**Definitions:**

| Terminology | Definition |
|---|---|
| **CMS** | Compliance Management System — the policies, procedures, and controls governing regulatory compliance |
| **Staff Member(s)** | An employee or external worker hired by the Company who supports the operations covered by this Policy |

---

## Compliance & Enforcement

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Block, Inc. Roles:**

- **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO):** Owns the CMS Policy; reports to the Board on compliance matters
- **Compliance Team:** Monitors adherence; provides reporting to executive management and the Board

**Subsidiary Roles:**

- **Subsidiary Compliance Leads:** Implement CMS requirements within their respective entities

### Monitoring and Reporting

On an ongoing basis, the Policy Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance and objective achievement. Compliance provides regular updates to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management.

### Record Retention

All documents are retained according to the longer of Block's Data Classification & Handling Policy or applicable regulatory requirements.

### Exceptions

Exception requests must be documented in writing, include justification and mitigating controls, and be submitted to the Policy Governance Team. See `exceptions/exceptions.jsonl` for the tamper-evident exception log.
