## Overview

**Introduction and Purpose:** This Standard establishes the Know Your Business (KYB) program for Cash App's Cash for Business product, governing merchant due diligence, onboarding, and ongoing monitoring requirements for US business customers.

**Scope:** Applies to Cash App US and its Cash for Business product line.

**Definitions:**

| Terminology | Definition |
|---|---|
| **KYB** | Know Your Business — the process of verifying the identity and legitimacy of business customers |
| **Beneficial Owner** | An individual who owns 25% or more of a legal entity customer |
| **CDD** | Customer Due Diligence — the process of identifying and verifying customer information |
| **Staff Member(s)** | An employee or external worker hired by the Company who supports the operations covered by this Standard |

---

## [Brand/Entity]-Specific Requirements

### Cash App

Cash for Business KYB requirements apply to all business accounts opened through the Cash App platform in the United States, including sole proprietors, partnerships, LLCs, and corporations.

## Compliance & Enforcement

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Block, Inc. Roles:**

- **Financial Crimes Compliance (FCC) Team:** Owns global KYB/KYC standards; provides oversight and guidance
- **Chief Compliance Officer:** Escalation for material compliance issues

**Subsidiary Roles:**

- **Cash App US Compliance Lead:** Primary owner of this Standard; manages KYB program implementation
- **Operations Team:** Executes KYB reviews, document collection, and adverse media screening

### Monitoring and Reporting

On an ongoing basis, the Standard Owner monitors KYB program metrics including approval rates, escalation volumes, and regulatory examination findings. Quarterly reporting is provided to Cash App US leadership and Block FCC.

### Record Retention

All KYB records are retained for a minimum of 5 years from account closure, or longer as required by FinCEN regulations or applicable state law.

### Exceptions

Exception requests must be documented in writing, include justification and mitigating controls, and be submitted to the Policy Governance Team. Material exceptions require FCC leadership approval.
